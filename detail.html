For example, the x and y

coordinate of a point are predicted by adding their delta in the preceding two points to the x and y of the last point, whereas the difference between subsequent points’ time values is expressed as a multiple of the most common increment, while RGB valued are compressed with simple difference coding. In each case the prediction residuals are compressed with adaptive arithmetic coding.

The compressor treats the LAS points of types 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 as consisting of a number of items: POINT10, GPSTIME11, RGB12, and WAVEPACKET13 and uses separate modules with separate version numbers to compress each item. This makes it easy to improve, for example, the compressor for the gps_time without affecting the other compression schemes while guaranteeing full backwards compatibility. More importantly it allows the engine to add new LAS point types as the LAS specification evolves into the future without affecting existing point types.
